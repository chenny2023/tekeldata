## Why a Withdrawal Stuck for 3 Years Usually Means Insolvency

A withdrawal stuck in processing for a few hours or days can be a technical glitch or network congestion. A withdrawal stuck for almost 3 years with zero help from support is a different scenario entirely. In the crypto casino space, when a platform indefinitely pauses withdrawals and goes silent, it is almost always a symptom of insolvency. The operator has likely run out of liquidity and is using the "processing" status as a stalling tactic while hoping for new deposits to cover the deficit. At this stage, waiting for a support ticket to be answered is not a viable strategy. You need to look at the blockchain to see if the money is actually still there.

## How to Independently Verify a Casino's Solvency

To understand if a platform like CryptoTabnet can actually pay you, you have to bypass their front-end interface and look directly at their on-chain wallets. Here is how you can verify their actual financial health:

1. **Locate the Operator's Main Wallets:** You need to identify the public blockchain addresses where the casino holds its operational funds and user deposits. 
2. **Read the Proof of Reserves:** Check the actual wallet balances against the platform's stated user liabilities. If a platform claims to have millions in user deposits but their on-chain wallets are nearly empty, they are insolvent.
3. **Monitor for Sudden Outflows:** Watch the wallet activity for sudden, large transfers to unknown wallets. A rapid drain of reserves often precedes a complete exit scam or a public announcement of insolvency.
